Intro

I am a sophomore Computer Science student at Virginia Tech with a large interest in data science, space, and quantitative finance. I am fascinated by how artificial intelligence will be used in the future of finance and investing. For ENGL 3844, I have written several Medium posts regarding my experience working for a local technology startup. I have also been a part of a group project where we made a podcast and a video about the future of artificial intelligence and technology. I am also a member of the Blacksburg startup, Abstract Assembly, working for them has inspired most of my blog posts for this class, feel free to check us out on their website. Feel free to check out my blog posts!

Blog Posts

I currently intern for the local Blacksburg startup, Abstract Assembly, and my first blog post reflects the benefits and difficlties that arise while working for a (mostly) online startup.

As a computer science major, I do a lot of programming. So I wanted to write an article about why it's important for everyone to know the basics of programming and coding no matter discipline they are in.I believe having these skills will make anyone extremely more valuable and be better prepared for the future of their careers.

Working for Abstract Assembly has been a truly remarkable experience for me. I feel not enough people get to be a part of a company at such an early of a stage. I wanted to write a blog post about some of the things I've learned from the startup, specifically why the culture of a startup is so important to the company's future.

Podcast

My group decided to conduct a podcast on rogue artificial intelligence and how that is viewed by society. We also decided to dive into exactly how and why these A.I. went rogue in the first place as well as what needs to be addressed so it remains unbiased. We discussed several occurences of rogue A.I., be sure to check it out!

Video

My group decided to make a video that looks into how people in our society view the future of technology. We wanted to see a variety of answers from people in the technology industry as well as from people who are not involved in the industry directly.
